Middle Point Landfill Hit With New Violations — Here's What It Means for You

Rutherford County's most controversial landfill just got cited again. We break down the news — and explain why your junk removal jobs aren't going anywhere.

By Lawson Gregory  |  Trash Titans Junk Removal  |  April 1, 2026  |  Murfreesboro, TN
📰 This article references reporting by WSMV Channel 4 News (March 31, 2026). All facts and quotes credited to their reporting.

If you've been following the ongoing saga of Middle Point Landfill out on the edge of Rutherford County, you already know it's been a headache for thousands of Murfreesboro residents. Now, as reported by WSMV Channel 4, the facility has been hit with a fresh round of violations — and the community group Rutherford Forward is pushing back hard against plans to make the already-troubled site even bigger.

As a junk removal company that hauls regularly throughout Murfreesboro, Smyrna, La Vergne, Nashville, and the rest of Middle Tennessee, we get asked about this stuff all the time. So let's talk about it honestly.

What the Violations Are

According to WSMV's reporting, the Tennessee Department of Environment and Conservation issued multiple new violations at Middle Point on March 9, 2026. Community organization Rutherford Forward made the violations public over the weekend.

⚠ New TDEC Violations — March 9, 2026
  • Unsatisfactory litter control — windblown waste observed spreading beyond the containment area
  • Unsatisfactory initial cover — waste not being properly covered per state requirements
  • Ongoing monitoring failures related to leachate conditions — the liquid that forms when waste breaks down

These violations come on the heels of years of growing community frustration. As WSMV noted, the city of Murfreesboro logged its 4,000th odor complaint about the facility back in October 2025 — prompting Mayor Shane McFarland to call it one of the worst-run landfills in the country.

"This is not an isolated incident — this is a pattern."

— Melissa Farley, Rutherford Forward (via WSMV)

The Expansion Fight

On top of the violations, BFI Waste Systems — the operator — is pushing to vertically expand the landfill by another 70 feet. That would add an estimated 19 million additional tons of waste capacity on top of an already struggling site. A Davidson County Chancery Court ruling in February allowed Murfreesboro officials to pause processing that application, but the fight isn't over.

Rutherford Forward's position is clear: expansion shouldn't even be on the table until the existing problems are fixed. Most Murfreesboro residents seem to agree.

Why This Story Matters to Middle Tennessee

The Middle Point situation is bigger than just a landfill getting citations. It's really a story about what happens when communities grow fast — like Murfreesboro has — and waste infrastructure doesn't keep pace with responsible management. Rutherford County is one of the fastest-growing counties in Tennessee, and that growth produces a lot of junk, construction debris, and household waste that needs to go somewhere.

The right answer isn't to pile more waste onto a site that's already struggling to comply with basic state standards. It's to invest in better facilities, better operations, and — for homeowners and businesses — smarter disposal choices.

That's part of why local junk removal matters. When you hire a licensed, insured hauler like Trash Titans, you're not just getting your garage cleaned out. You're putting that disposal decision in the hands of someone who is accountable for where that stuff ends up and how it gets there.

What You Can Do

If you're a Murfreesboro or Rutherford County resident who has concerns about Middle Point Landfill, here are a few things worth doing:

Stay informed. Follow Rutherford Forward and WSMV's ongoing coverage. This story is moving fast and decisions are being made at the county and state level that will affect the whole community.

File complaints. The City of Murfreesboro has an odor complaint line specifically for Middle Point issues. Every complaint creates a paper trail that regulators and courts pay attention to.

Choose responsible haulers. When you hire someone to remove junk, ask where it goes. A reputable company should be able to tell you. At Trash Titans, we're happy to answer that question.
